## MISSION #1 — MARS
**The Red Planet | 4th Planet from the Sun | 140 Million Miles from Earth (average)**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Size** | About half the size of Earth — but same amount of dry land (no oceans) |
| **Temperature** | Average -80°F. Can drop to -220°F at night near the poles |
| **Day Length** | 24 hours and 37 minutes — almost exactly like Earth |
| **Cool Stat** | Olympus Mons on Mars is 3x taller than Mount Everest |

---

### MISSION BRIEFING

*Incoming transmission from Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter:*

"Mars has the largest dust storms in the solar system. They can cover the entire planet and last for months. During the 2018 global storm, the Opportunity rover — which ran on solar power — lost contact with Earth and never recovered. It had been operating for 15 years."

## MISSION #2 — JUPITER
**The Giant | 5th Planet from the Sun | 365-500 Million Miles from Earth**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Size** | 1,300 Earths could fit inside Jupiter |
| **Moons** | 95 confirmed moons — the most of any planet |
| **The Great Red Spot** | A storm that has lasted at least 350 years — wider than Earth |
| **Cool Stat** | Jupiter spins faster than any other planet — a day lasts only 10 hours |

---

### MISSION BRIEFING

*Incoming transmission from Juno spacecraft:*

"Jupiter has no solid surface. If you flew toward it, you'd pass through layers of gas that get denser and hotter until the pressure crushes anything we've ever built. The deepest our probes have gone before being destroyed is about 100 miles in — and Jupiter is 43,000 miles wide."

---

### ASTRONAUT CHALLENGES

**1.** Jupiter's moon Europa has a liquid ocean under its ice surface — scientists think it might have life. Design a robot to drill through the ice and explore. What features does it need?

**2.** You're orbiting Jupiter in a spacecraft. The Great Red Spot is directly below you. What do you see, hear, and feel?

**3.** If Jupiter became a star (it's almost big enough), how would life on Earth change? Name 3 effects.

## MISSION #3 — INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION (ISS)
**Earth Orbit | 250 Miles Above the Surface | Speed: 17,500 mph**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Size** | Bigger than a football field — the largest structure ever built in space |
| **Orbits** | Completes 16 orbits of Earth every single day |
| **Built By** | 15 countries working together over 13 years (1998-2011) |
| **Cool Stat** | Astronauts see 16 sunrises and 16 sunsets every 24 hours |

---

### MISSION BRIEFING

*Welcome to the ISS. Current crew: 7 astronauts.*

"Everything floats. Water doesn't pour — it forms spheres. Sleeping is done in a bag strapped to the wall. Exercise is mandatory for 2 hours every day, or your muscles start disappearing within weeks. The view from the cupola window is the most photographed thing astronauts talk about when they return home."

---

### ASTRONAUT CHALLENGES

**1.** You have one 90-minute window to look out the cupola window as the ISS orbits Earth. What 3 places do you hope to fly over and why?

**2.** Astronauts on the ISS must exercise 2 hours per day to prevent muscle loss. Design a 30-minute workout routine that works in zero gravity.

**3.** You've been in space 6 months. You finally land back on Earth. What's the first thing you do? The first thing you eat?
