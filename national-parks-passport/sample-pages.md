## PARK #1 — YELLOWSTONE NATIONAL PARK
**Wyoming, Montana, Idaho | Est. 1872 | World's First National Park**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Size** | 3,472 square miles (bigger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined) |
| **Famous For** | Old Faithful — a geyser that erupts every 44 to 125 minutes |
| **Wildlife** | Bison, grizzly bears, gray wolves, elk, and bald eagles |
| **Cool Fact** | Yellowstone sits on top of a supervolcano — the ground itself is alive |

---

### DID YOU KNOW?

There are more geysers in Yellowstone than anywhere else on Earth — over 500 of them. Old Faithful isn't even the biggest. Steamboat Geyser can shoot water 300 feet into the air!

---

### ADVENTURE PROMPTS

**1.** If you could name a brand-new geyser discovered in Yellowstone, what would you call it — and why?

**2.** You're on a hike and spot a grizzly bear 200 feet away. What are your three steps?

**3.** Rangers say the ground in some parts of Yellowstone is thin enough to break through to boiling water below. Design a warning sign that would actually make a kid stop and pay attention.

## PARK #2 — GRAND CANYON NATIONAL PARK
**Arizona | Est. 1919 | One of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Size** | 1,902 square miles — the canyon itself is 277 miles long |
| **Depth** | Over a mile deep (you could stack 4 Empire State Buildings inside it) |
| **Age** | The oldest exposed rock layers are nearly 2 billion years old |
| **Cool Fact** | The Colorado River at the bottom looks tiny from the rim — it's actually 300 feet wide |

---

### DID YOU KNOW?

The Grand Canyon isn't just one color. As the sun moves across the sky, the canyon walls shift from orange to red to purple to gold. Early morning is when the colors are most intense — photographers sometimes camp overnight just to catch the first light.

---

### ADVENTURE PROMPTS

**1.** Scientists can read 2 billion years of Earth's history in the canyon walls like pages in a book. If the layers were chapters, what would you name the chapter you'd most want to read?

**2.** You've been given one hour at the canyon — alone, no phone. What do you do?

**3.** Mules carry visitors down the canyon trail. Design a better vehicle for getting to the bottom. Rules: no motors, must be safe for kids.

## PARK #3 — YOSEMITE NATIONAL PARK
**California | Est. 1890 | Home of the Tallest Waterfall in North America**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Size** | 1,169 square miles — about the size of Rhode Island |
| **Famous For** | El Capitan — a 3,000-foot vertical granite wall that rock climbers attempt to scale |
| **Waterfalls** | Yosemite Falls drops 2,425 feet — taller than the Eiffel Tower |
| **Cool Fact** | Giant sequoia trees here are over 2,000 years old and wider than a school bus |

---

### DID YOU KNOW?

In February, if conditions are perfect, the setting sun lights up Horsetail Fall on El Capitan so it glows bright orange — like a river of fire pouring down the cliff. It only happens for about two weeks a year, and people travel from all over the world to see it.

---

### ADVENTURE PROMPTS

**1.** Free solo climbers climb El Capitan with no ropes — just their hands and feet. It takes about 4 hours at the world-record pace. Would you ever try it? What would be the hardest part?

**2.** A giant sequoia tree has been alive since before the Roman Empire. If you could ask it one question about history, what would you ask?

**3.** Yosemite Valley is surrounded on three sides by 3,000-foot walls. Design the ultimate one-day adventure in the valley — what do you do from sunrise to sunset?
