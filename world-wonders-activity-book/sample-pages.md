## WONDER #1 — THE GREAT WALL OF CHINA
**China | Asia | Built: 7th Century BC to 17th Century AD**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Total Length** | Over 13,000 miles — long enough to circle Earth halfway |
| **Built By** | Millions of workers over 2,000 years, including soldiers and prisoners |
| **Height** | Up to 30 feet tall, wide enough for 10 soldiers to walk side by side |
| **Cool Stat** | Sticky rice was used as mortar — it made the wall stronger than stone alone |

---

### EXPLORER BRIEFING

The Great Wall is not one wall — it's hundreds of walls built by different emperors, connected or overlapping. Some sections have crumbled completely. Others are so well-preserved they look like they were built last year. About 30% of the original wall has disappeared over time.

---

### THINK LIKE AN EXPLORER

**1.** The wall took over 2,000 years to build. If you were an emperor starting construction today, how would you build it faster? What technology would you use?

**2.** Some sections of the wall are so steep they're more like staircases than walkways. Guards had to patrol them in all weather, all year. Design the ideal guard post with everything a soldier would need.

**3.** Historians believe hundreds of thousands of workers are buried inside the wall itself. If you could find and read one worker's diary from 500 BC, what do you hope it would say?

## WONDER #2 — MACHU PICCHU
**Peru | South America | Built: ~1450 AD | Elevation: 7,970 feet**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Built By** | The Inca Empire — without wheels, iron tools, or mortar |
| **Location** | Hidden in the Andes Mountains, above the clouds |
| **Lost For** | 400 years — rediscovered by a Western explorer in 1911 |
| **Cool Stat** | The stones fit so perfectly together that a knife blade cannot slide between them |

---

### EXPLORER BRIEFING

Machu Picchu wasn't "discovered" by Hiram Bingham in 1911. Local Peruvian farmers had been living near it and knew it existed. What Bingham did was introduce it to the outside world. The Inca people who built it abandoned it about 100 years after construction — nobody knows exactly why. Theories include disease, Spanish conquest, or a planned retreat.

---

### THINK LIKE AN EXPLORER

**1.** The Inca moved 50-ton stone blocks up steep mountain slopes without wheels or metal tools. Engineers still aren't sure exactly how. What's your best theory?

**2.** Machu Picchu was hidden above the clouds for 400 years. If you were exploring a jungle and suddenly saw it from below, what would be your first move?

**3.** The site has an "Intihuatana stone" — a carved rock that works as a precise solar calendar. How would you use the sun to tell the time or the season without any instruments?

## WONDER #3 — THE PYRAMIDS OF GIZA
**Egypt | Africa | Built: ~2560 BC | Age: Over 4,500 years old**

---

### FAST FACTS

| | |
|---|---|
| **Height** | The Great Pyramid stands 481 feet — tallest man-made structure for 3,800 years |
| **Blocks** | 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing 2.5 to 15 tons |
| **Built By** | Skilled workers — not slaves (new evidence shows they were paid and housed nearby) |
| **Cool Stat** | The pyramid is aligned with true north to within 0.05 degrees — more accurate than the Paris meridian |

---

### EXPLORER BRIEFING

We still don't fully know how the pyramids were built. We know workers used copper tools, wooden sleds, and ramps — but moving 2.3 million blocks in about 20 years means a new block was placed every 2 minutes, every day, for two decades. Engineers and archaeologists are still working it out.

---

### THINK LIKE AN EXPLORER

**1.** The pharaoh Khufu wanted to be remembered forever. The pyramid has lasted 4,500 years. What would you build today that might still exist in the year 6,500 AD?

**2.** Workers placed one stone block every 2 minutes for 20 straight years. How would you organize a team of workers to do that without modern equipment?

**3.** Inside the Great Pyramid are hidden chambers still being scanned with cosmic ray technology. If a secret room was found tomorrow, what do you hope is inside?
